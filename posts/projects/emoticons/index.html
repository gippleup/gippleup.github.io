How I started Programming(1) - Emoticon

Apr 08, 2020

My Journey to programming started when I started making emoticon.

It was a thing that I was tempted to, but I refrained to do so, for long time. How long? Like, two-weeks?

It was summer, 2017, maybe late May, I took time off from school. At that time, I was too tired learning stuffs that I barely wanted to learn. I wanted something that can inspire me, something that I can immerse myself in.

At that time, emoticon was hot. I mean, really hot, at least in Korea. Many success stories about emoticon was spread and I wanted to be part of the story, though I didn’t admit.

I asked myself.

Month 1: “Yeah, it’s super fun!“

⬆️ This was a thing. With this sticker pack, I could earn some pocket money and confidence. And this, was a thing that I reminded time to time when I wanted to give up making emoticon.


Afterwards, I made about 10 or more emoticon packs in a row, which were all failure.


Month 2: “Why didn’t I try this sooner?!“

White Seal, Nora

It was really fun. I was fascinated how an animation can be made with a program. I learnt after effects for making animatons, and I could learn several tools - like joystick n slider, duik, element3D, etc - which were super fun to learn.


To look back, I was fascinated by a program. I thought, 'even this boring and loose process can be super efficient with a program.' I could see that I'm learning something and getting better and better. It was fuel for me.
