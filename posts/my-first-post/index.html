Start

Mar 15, 2020

It’s been a month since I started learning programming.

It's way too fun learning writing codes. From the beginning of the course, I knew that I found the right track that I can pleasurably run around.


Important lessons are...

  1. Don't rush! It's time-efficient.
  2. Learn from others, it's alse time-efficient.
  3. Think before coding, it's also time-efficient.

Time, that's all that matters.

Before starting bootcamp, I've spent one year or more for making something like this.

Making those kind of thing was fun, but the result was not good. I tried to publish these emoticons to one of a main platform, all rejected. It was hard to endure. My proposal had been rejected about 20 times.

Then, I learnt that I may not be successful on emoticon market. And I learnt how to make an character animation using after effects. Cool.

What's next? I thought, while playing league of legends. I enjoyed playing game. And on second thought, I decided to make a game.

It took 1 year. It took 11 months more than I expected. 2 weeks for giving up, 3 months turning back to emoticon, another 3 months passed for trials and errors, 2 month for healing my exhausted body and mind. And 3 months for making the game.

result? Another good lesson, which didn't produce successful output.


However...

I finally found my way, being a programmer. Though it took looooong time, I found my way. That was enough for me.

Plus, the life pattern that I lived for last 2 years suits for bootcampt, super great. (Wake up, work, take a walk, eat lunch, take a walk, work, eat dinner, exercise for survival, work, meditate, shower, sleep.)

Argh...!

well, this wasn't what I wanted to write. Actually, I didn't plan to write a post today. But, this is also fun...! Though I didn't wrap up this clearly, I should go to sleep now. The clock points AM 00:14.Time to go to sleep, for a new born programmer... Zzz
